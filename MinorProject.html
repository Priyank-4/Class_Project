Historical Places of India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Hampi

Hampi is an ancient village in the northern part of Karnataka state, India. It was once the glorious capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century. The ruins of Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are spread over a vast area and include numerous temples, palaces, and other structures.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi, India. It was constructed in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan as the palace for his new capital, Shahjahanabad. The Red Fort, with its impressive red sandstone walls, is now a popular tourist attraction and a designated UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves are a group of ancient rock-cut temples located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. These remarkable caves, carved out of the Charanandri Hills, showcase a fusion of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain religious art and architecture. Ellora Caves have been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a magnificent palace situated in the city of Mysore, Karnataka, India. Built in the Indo-Saracenic architectural style, it is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and a major tourist attraction. The palace is renowned for its grandeur and is illuminated with thousands of lights during special occasions.

Khajuraho Temples

The Khajuraho Temples are a group of ancient Hindu and Jain temples located in the Chhatarpur district of Madhya Pradesh, India. These temples, built between the 10th and 12th centuries, are famous for their exquisite carvings depicting various aspects of life, including sensuous sculptures. The Khajuraho Temples are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
